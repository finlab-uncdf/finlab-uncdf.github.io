Design Challenge

30 Min

About

Defining a ‘Design Challenge’ is a good way to have the participants converge their efforts by working together to crafting a series of statements to use as the brief for the ideation and prototyping phases.

Use Cases

  • Clarifying the objective of the design process.
  • Building consensus within the team.

Limitations

This activity requires time to think through the research and might take several rounds of refinement.

Understand

  • Defining a Design Challenge is an organic process that can be hard to formalise. It is recommended to clearly visualise the thought process even if it may feel a little redundant.
  • The ‘Solution Goal’ should capture the intended impact and who it will benefit. To be relevant, any solution brainstormed beyond this point should directly address the Solution Goal statement.
  • The ‘Opportunity’ statements will convey possible strategies to leverage in order to meet the Solution Goal using How Might We statements. Some HMWs may have already been created in a previous session, if so those should be taken into account as well.
  • The ‘Design Challenge’ should be a prioritised Opportunity statement that the team wishes to solution for over the remainder of the exercise.

Step by Step

1. Consolidate the challenges: See how the various challenges identified during earlier discussions or research can be brought together in a single intent statement or Solution Goal.
2. Formulate opportunities: Translate the Solution Goal into a series of possible strategies or Opportunities to move into ideation (you’ll recognise the same HMW statements as used in the Insights and Opportunities activity).
3. Prioritise key opportunity areas: Build consensus around a single Key Opportunity statement or Design Challenge to make sure the team aligns on the direction.

References

Facilitators Section

How to for Facilitators

  1. At the start: Make sure everyone understands the various terms in the template.
  2. During the exercise: Help the participants draw from their previous discussions, this should be an opportunity to reflect on and synthesise existing work rather than finding new ideas.

Facilitators Question Bank

  • Does the team have a clear idea of what they are trying to achieve?
  • What problem are you solving for and for whom?
  • What HMWs are you gravitating towards? Why?
  • Do any other HMWs come to mind?
  • Are these HMWs actionable? Should they be made more specific or more broad?
  • Which HMW do you feel is the strongest?
  • How well does this statement align with your Design Principles?

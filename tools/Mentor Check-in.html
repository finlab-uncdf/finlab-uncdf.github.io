Mentor Check-in

60 Min

About

The Mentor Check-In allows for participants to receive input from industry professionals who have faced similar challenges and can inform them on a variety of aspects of their work be it business strategy, scaling or creating a robust MVP.

Use Cases

  • Getting expert opinions on tough challenges the participants are facing.
  • Better understanding one's strategy and possible alternatives.

Limitations

Organising Mentor Check-ins or knowledge shares will require time on the part of the organiser to make sure each participant gets paired with a relevant mentor who will give them valuable input.

Understand

  • The following template is only a recommendation for a typical flow with a mentor. 'Introduce yourself' and your role at your company.
  • Make sure to be mindful of the time alloted and decide how to best 'Present your product or service': should it be a one minute pitch or a longer fifteen minutes presentation?
  • Prepare a few questions you'd like to 'Collect feedback' on and expect more questions and input from the mentors. The last part often runs short so make sure you factor in enough time for a fruitful discussion after presenting your solution.

Step by Step

  1. Do your research: Before chosing a mentor to speak to, see which ones are most likely to have contructive input for you and that their expertise is relevant.
  2. Set up the call: Whether in person or online, make sure the mentor is available and has all the information they need about you. Many will want to see a business plan or a short deck before meeting you. On your end make sure to a have a few key questions you'd like to ask.
  3. Keep in touch: If a valuable relationship starts, make sure to keep them in the loop of your activity and check in regularly.

Facilitators Section

How to for Facilitators

  1. Prior to the session: Make sure each participant has been able to secure a session with a mentor. This may take a bit of coordination and back and forth on scheduling.
  2. During the check-in: If this is part of the workshop session and several participants will be speaking to the mentors one after the other, make sure that everyone is on top on time and sticking to their dedicated scheduled slot.
  3. When summarising: Have participants share the feedback they received.

Facilitators Question Bank

  • What would you like input on? Your business plan? How to go to market and scale?
  • Who is best suited to provide constructive input? Are they available?
  • Has everyone chosen a timeslot?

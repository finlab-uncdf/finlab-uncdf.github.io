Wireframing

60 Min.

About

Wireframing helps the team break down and visualise the full set of screens a user will see and use to access the service being designed.

Use Cases

  • Prototyping and testing the digital experience being designed.
  • Identifying redundencies or painpoints in the latest iteration of the service.
  • Assessing the scope of work needed to take the idea forward.

Limitations

This activity is specifically intended for digital products or services that rely on screens for service delivery.

Understand

  • Each phone outline can be used to quickly draw what would be displayed in the app at each stage. The screens can then be connected together.

Step by Step

1. Outline the key steps: Start by dicussing as a team the key user interactions.
2. Visualise the touchpoints: For each step, identify all the screens necessary to bring the concept to life.
3. Connect the screens: Draw the connections between different screens and how a button might lead to the next screen.
4. Refine the flow: Run throught he wireframes with different User Personas in mind and see if the flow is intuitive or if it needs refinement.

Facilitators Section

How to for Facilitators

  1. At the start: Make sure the team has a clear understanding of the key steps of their service.
  2. During the exercise: Make sure participants are being detailed enough in understanding the flow from screen to screen.
  3. When summarising: Have participants present the Wireframe flow to another group and see if it is self-explanatory.

Facilitators Question Bank

  • Are all the steps of the experience represented here? Are there any missing screens?
  • Don't worry about making it pretty, instead focus on blocking out the key interactions and features necessary on each screen.
  • Are you able to clearly connect the screens together to create a folw from the begining of the experience to the end?

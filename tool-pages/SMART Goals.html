SMART Goals

30 Min

SMART goals help teams take think through their goals and make sure they are actionable and achievable.

USE CASES

  • Build consensus within a team on next steps.
  • Define clear goals that team members can be held accountable to.

LIMTATIONS

SMART goals should be defined following a strategic exercise. The tool is best used to build detail around a team's current goals, and not as a way to identify new goals.

SMART Goals | DecisionSkills

UNDERSTANDING THE TOOL

  • ‘Specific’ is the box in which to clearly define the nature or what of the goal.
  • ‘Measurable’ focuses on metrics that help track progress on the goal.
  • ‘Achievable’ should describe feasible actions to achieve the goal.
  • ‘Relevant’ should answer why the goal is it relevant to the team.
  • ‘Time-Bound’ should factor in time constraints - short and long term - and reflect the overall priorities for the team.

STEP BY STEP

  1. Pick a goal or set of goals: Take the nearest goals in your Activity Plan and use this worksheet to clarify them.
  2. Clarify: Break down each goal according to the five components in the left column.
  3. Get buy-in: Discuss the goals with the team and challenge each other to make sure they are feasible.

HOW TO FOR FACILITATORS

  1. At the start: Help participants think through the way they are defining goals. Use previously completed activity planning or roadmapping to guide discussion.
  2. During the exercise: Help participants formulate SMART goals and identify challenges in meeting them.
  3. When summarising: Have participants share goals to make sure everyone is on the same page.

FACILITATORS QUESTION BANK

  • How do you set goals in your team?
  • How would SMART goals be different from what you have been doing?
  • What goals or milestones have you identified so far?
  • Are there any other shorter-term goals to address?
  • How achievable are you goals? Do you have the right resources (staff, time, money) to pull them off?
  • How to these goals map to your overall strategy as a business or organisation?

HUMAN CENTERED DESIGN | IDEATION

First Idea + Crazy Idea

15 Min

A good warm up to more structured ideation, this tool helps people voice their top of mind ideas - both seemingly logical and crazy. The tool is helpful to get people involved, to get them excited, and to clear their mind so that they can be ready to problem solve further.

USE CASES

  • Warm up before more detailed ideation sessions
  • Document ideas that people are simple and obvious
  • Set the tone for creative, fun brainstorming

LIMITATIONS

This activity is meant to be short and fun. Consider other ideation formats for longer brainstorming sessions.

Brainstorming Techniques | Sprouts

UNDERSTANDING THE TOOL

  • ‘First Ideas’ should consist of ideas that might feel obvious or simplistic but ring true to the team in terms of feasibility and desirability.
  • ‘Crazy Ideas’ should consist of playful, moonshot, big bold ideas that can get people excited and also set the tone in terms of what could be possible if the team were to think radically.

STEP BY STEP

  1. Ideate: This warm-up to the ideation can happen first as individuals, and then as team where each participant gets a chance to add new ideas, and build on someone else's ideas. Each individual should ideally come up with 1-2 ideas that are seemingly logical (first ideas) and 1-2 crazy ideas.
  2. Discuss the Ideas: Before wrapping up the exercise, review the ideas and see if there are some clear themes emerging - park them aside to come back to later.

HOW TO FOR FACILITATORS

  1. At the start: Set the context for the activity. Start with two sheets of paper - one with the header ‘First Ideas’ and the other ‘Crazy Ideas’.
  2. During the exercise: Get the participants to think big and encourage playful and practical ideas alike. Get participants to park their ideas under the relevant header.
  3. When summarising: Have participants explain their ideas, and help them identify interesting themes and learnings from the session.

FACILITATORS QUESTION BANK

  • What problem are you solving for and for whom?
  • What HMWs are you gravitating towards? Why?
  • Who has the most number of crazy ideas?
  • Who is your top 1-2 ideas as individuals?
  • What are some themes that may be emerging in the ideas?
